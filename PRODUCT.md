# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Single user — the owner, a lifelong Buffalo Bills / Cleveland Guardians / Cleveland
Cavaliers fan tracking every professional sporting event he attends in person. No
login; one person's private almanac behind an obscure URL. The context is dual:

1. **Lean-back browse** — at a desk, reviewing history, records, and the collection.
2. **In-the-moment log** — on a phone at or right after a game, on stadium wifi,
   recording what he just watched.

The interface must serve both: the reflective browse *and* the one-handed,
flaky-connection log.

## Product Purpose

A personal record of every pro sporting event attended, enriched automatically from
ESPN and gamified into a lifelong collection. It answers **"what have I seen?"** with
authority: personal W/L record when attending, attended-win streaks, biggest blowouts
and closest games witnessed, playoff games, teams collected, stadiums visited.

Success = the dataset is complete and correct (every game's real score and venue
present), opening the app delivers an immediate, satisfying read on the collection, and
the personal game notes — the memories that give the data meaning — are never lost
under the numbers.

## Brand Personality

A **stats command center** with a sports-desk sensibility. Confident, precise,
data-forward — records and streaks land at-a-glance the moment it loads. The voice is a
sharp beat writer: factual, economical, occasionally dry, never breathless.

Three words: **precise, authoritative, personal.** Think a private
ESPN-meets-Letterboxd built by someone who cares equally about the numbers and the
memories.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI-SaaS:** gradients, glassmorphism, identical icon→heading→text card
  grids, inline badge soup. (Called out explicitly in the project brief — avoid all
  three.)
- **Team-color wallpaper:** brand colors as full-bleed backgrounds. Team color is a
  data-driven accent, never the canvas.
- **Fantasy-sports maximalism** (ESPN/Yahoo fantasy consoles): cramped, ad-dense,
  everything-shouting.
- **Warm scrapbook / parchment nostalgia:** this is a command center, not a memory box.
  Warmth comes from the notes and team color, not sepia and paper textures.

## Design Principles

- **The numbers carry authority.** Records, scores, and streaks are the product.
  Typography treats figures as first-class: tabular, aligned, instantly scannable.
- **Team color is a data channel, not decoration.** Every accent earns its hue from the
  team or league it represents. Color means something, or it's absent.
- **Collection is a feeling.** Seen/unseen and visited/unvisited read at a glance and
  feel satisfying to complete. The almanac should invite the next game.
- **The memory survives the metric.** Notes are the soul; surface them, never bury them.
- **One-handed truth.** It works on a phone at the game — thumb-reachable logging,
  honest pending / needs-review states, nothing that assumes a mouse or a good signal.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Single-user, but built to quality. WCAG **AA** contrast (≥4.5:1 body, ≥3:1 large text and
UI). **Never convey seen/unseen or win/loss by team color alone** — pair with fill state,
label, or icon (team palettes are full of red/green and other colorblind-ambiguous
pairs). Full keyboard operability, visible focus rings, and a genuine
`prefers-reduced-motion` path for any count-ups, reveals, or map animations.
